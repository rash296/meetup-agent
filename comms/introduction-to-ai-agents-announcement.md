# Introduction to AI Agents: From Chatbots to Autonomous Workflows

**You've used ChatGPT. Maybe you've built a chatbot. But do you actually know what an AI agent is — and why everyone keeps saying they're the future?** Most explanations are either too hand-wavy or too deep in the weeds. We're going to fix that.

Join us for three talks that take you from "what even is an AI agent?" to "here's how you'd build one." We'll demystify the difference between a chatbot that answers questions and an agent that autonomously completes multi-step workflows — with live demos, real tool-use patterns, and honest opinions about what works and what's hype.

## What You'll Learn

You'll see how AI agents differ from simple chatbot interactions — not just in theory, but through live demos of autonomous workflows. We'll cover tool-use patterns (how agents call APIs, read files, and take actions), MCP integrations that give agents access to external systems, and the UX considerations that make agent interactions actually usable. You'll leave with a clear mental model of the agent landscape and concrete next steps for building your own.

## Your Speakers

**Sam Takahashi** is a robotics engineer turned AI agent developer who's passionate about building multi-step autonomous agent workflows. He'll open the evening by breaking down what agents actually are, how they reason through multi-step tasks, and why tool-use is the capability that makes them useful.

**Jordan Reeves** is a full-stack developer and open-source contributor who builds developer tooling powered by Claude and MCP integrations. He'll show you the infrastructure side — how MCP lets agents connect to tools, databases, and APIs in a standardized way.

**Alex Kim** is a UX engineer focused on making AI interactions intuitive and advocates for human-centered AI product design. He'll close with the design perspective — because an agent that works perfectly but confuses users is still a failed product.
