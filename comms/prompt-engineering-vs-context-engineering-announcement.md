# Prompt Engineering vs Context Engineering

**Still tweaking prompts one at a time and hoping for the best?** There's a better way — and it's time we had an honest conversation about when prompt engineering is enough and when you need to think bigger.

Join us for a panel debate where three local AI practitioners go head-to-head on the techniques that actually work: from carefully crafted one-shot prompts to full context engineering with CLAUDE.md files, MCP integrations, and agent architectures. This isn't a lecture — it's a real debate with real opinions.

## What You'll Get Out of This

You'll hear three practitioners argue — with examples — about where prompt engineering hits its ceiling and where context engineering picks up. Expect concrete comparisons: when is a well-crafted prompt all you need? When do you need CLAUDE.md files, tool-use patterns, or full agent architectures? ## Your Panelists

**Maria Chen** is an ML engineer at a Tucson defense contractor who specializes in evaluating LLM outputs for reliability and safety in critical systems. She'll bring the prompt engineering perspective — because when you're working in high-stakes environments, sometimes a precise prompt is exactly what you need.

**Jordan Reeves** is a full-stack developer and open-source contributor who builds developer tooling powered by Claude and MCP integrations. He lives and breathes context engineering daily, and he'll make the case for why structured context beats clever prompts at scale.

**Sam Takahashi** is a robotics engineer turned AI agent developer who builds multi-step autonomous agent workflows. He bridges both worlds — his work shows exactly where prompt engineering breaks down and structured tool-use becomes essential.
